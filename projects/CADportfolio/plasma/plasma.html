Plasma Cutter Nozzle

A sculptor at Tulane purchased a new size of nozzle for her plasma cutter, and the new nozzles didn't fit inside the existing holding ring. This is a project in progress, but thus far I have

  • measured all of the parts using calipers,
  • modeled and 3D printed rings corresponding to possible different fits,
  • and given them to her to see which one she likes the most.

Once she chooses a sizing, I will

  • cut a cylindrical piece of plastic to that size on the CNC lathe to ensure that the machining fit is also correct
  • and then use the conversational mode on it with the inner diameter turn and tool to machine out the required circumference.

I train MakerSpace users on the lathe, so it's great to have an opportunity to use it in a non-pedagogical setting.



The nozzle is slightly too large for the holding ring, as you can see below - when I measured, I found that the nozzle itself had a 25mm diameter, while the ring's hole was only 24mm.

I 3D printed three different ring sizes: one with the same size hole as the actual ring (to later machine out), one with a 25.5mm diameter, and one with a 26mm diameter. The 25.5mm diameter ring has a very snug fit, whereas the 26mm diameter ring is easier to remove. She's in the process of picking a sizing, but once she does I'll use the inner diameter turning tool and the existing inner diameter toolpath creation window on the Tormach lathe digital readout to cut the part to her desired diameter.
