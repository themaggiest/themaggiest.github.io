CNC Hotwire

Following this guide, my friend (a computer science major) and I built our own 4-axis CNC foam cutter. I

  • 3D printed the parts listed under the video,
  • assembled the basic structure, and
  • collaborated with my friend to make sure it ran smoothly

to cut our own radio control (RC) plane wings.



We volunteered to host an aeromodelling workshop together at Girls in STEM at Tulane (GIST), a semiannual program hosted by Tulane that brings middle school girls from New Orleans to our campus, and brought in our CNC-cut wings and hand-cut fuselages:

and used this machine as our proof of concept for proposing a plan to create a business specializing in custom RC planes for a pitch competition, where we won $3500 for the idea.

We were also named as finalists for the Entrepreneurship Pelican Cup and Tulane Innovation Institute $50K Demo Day.
