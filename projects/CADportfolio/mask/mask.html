Mardi Gras Mask

This year, my roommates and I decided to dress as the three colors of Mardi Gras for the Krewe of Muses. I wanted a masquerade mask that would fit under my glasses without the indignities of coating my face in papier-mâché, so I decided to 3D print one. For an as perfect as possible fit, I

  • scanned my face in Polycam,
  • used the resulting mesh to model a mask in Autodesk Fusion,
  • and printed the mask with a 0.08mm layer height for maximum detail.

I was able to wear my mask all night long with my glasses and wig over it with no difficulties.



I made a scan of my face with glasses on in Polycam to know both the geometry that I should match (the contours of my face) and the geometry that I should avoid (my glasses). With that first scan, I used Fusion's surfacing capabilities to create a lofted surface representing my face, and offset from that surface to make the actual mask surface.

Then I thickened the surface and removed holes from the mask to create a lace-like look. I also thickened some small surfaces for some raised sections.

In OrcaSlicer, I sliced the mask into 0.08mm layers for higher detail and printed it. Although I had to sand some of the ridges left by supports, it was as comfortable as a plastic mask under two layers of costuming could be.
